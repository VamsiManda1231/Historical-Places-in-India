Explore Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal

Taj Mahal

Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh

The Taj Mahal, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, is one of the most famous monuments in the world. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and an iconic symbol of India's rich history.

Red Fort

Red Fort

Location: Delhi

The Red Fort, a symbol of India's independence, was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century. It is an iconic monument that represents India's rich cultural heritage.

Hampi

Hampi

Location: Hampi, Karnataka

Hampi was once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is known for its ancient temples, ruins, and stunning landscapes.

Amber Fort

Amber Fort

Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan

Amber Fort, located in Jaipur, is a majestic fort built by Raja Man Singh in the 16th century. It is known for its artistic Hindu style elements and has large ramparts and a series of gates and cobbled paths.

Konark Sun Temple

Sun Temple

Location: Konark, Odisha

The Konark Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a 13th-century temple dedicated to the Sun God. It is known for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings.

Gateway of India

Gateway of India

Location: Mumbai, Maharashtra

The Gateway of India is a monumental arch built in the early 20th century in Mumbai. It commemorates the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911 and has since become an iconic symbol of Mumbai.

Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace

Location: Mysore, Karnataka

Mysore Palace is an iconic landmark in Karnataka. Built in the Indo-Saracenic style, it was the residence of the Wodeyar dynasty and is known for its grand architecture and beautiful gardens.

Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal

Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is a stunning five-story palace in Jaipur, Rajasthan. It was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh and is known for its unique architecture.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar

Location: Delhi

Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in the 12th century and is an important symbol of India's rich history.

Dwaraka

Dwaraka

Location: Gujarat

Dwaraka is an ancient city and a significant pilgrimage site in India. It is believed to be the legendary city founded by Lord Krishna, making it one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites.

Sanchi

Sanchi

Location: Hyderabad, Telangana

Charminar is a historic monument and mosque located in Hyderabad, Telangana. Built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, it is an iconic symbol of Hyderabad and an important cultural landmark.

Ellora Caves

Ellora Caves

Location: Aurangabad, Maharashtra

Ellora Caves, a series of 34 magnificent rock-cut temples in northwest-central Maharashtra state, western India. The caves are carved into the Sahyadri range of hills in northwest-central Maharashtra.
